Biography

I’m an environmental consultant specializing in water science and policy in Florida.

My main interests are in using data analysis and visualizations to help clients in the private, public, and nonprofit sectors fulfil their missions and goals while protecting water quality and human health.

In my spare time I enjoy cycling, hiking, and photography.

A longer story

I have a background in wetlab bench research. During my undergraduate studies, I was privileged to receive several awards and fellowships that allowed me to conduct research in laboratories while earning my degree. I worked on understanding cellular regulation and developmental biology in both plants and animals. Later I switched focus to the fascinating microscopic world, applying similar molecular methods to understanding the ecology of microbes living on corals and their relationship to human and reef health.

I’ve long been a proponent of science-based policies and evidence-based decision making, and saw an opportunity to engage in this process in a meaningful way by starting a consulting company, Frydenborg Ecologic. I started the company along with my dad in 2014, and I now find myself working at the intersection of policy and science. Our company has worked with private and public stakeholders on projects concerning Numeric Nutrient Criteria, Environmental Resource Permitting, Waste Water Treatment Facility permits, NPDES compliance, Microbial Source Tracking, policy reviews and meta-analyses, and ecological restoration. I find the work consistently interesting, challenging, and rewarding. I get to work on complex problems that have time and resource restraints, clear consequence and impact, and controversy driven by non-scientific ideology. There are very few boring days.
